About me

Hi! I’m Nikita Fastovets — a passionate full-stack developer and Computer Science student at KNEU. I enjoy building modern web applications using FastAPI, PostgreSQL, and WordPress, and I love creating clean, functional interfaces that actually make sense. I’m currently developing Qurvey, a survey and analytics platform, where I handle both backend and frontend logic. My goal is to keep learning, improving, and turning ideas into real working products. Outside of coding, I train in Taekwon-Do ITF, explore new business concepts, and experiment with tech gear. This blog is where I share my experiences, thoughts, and lessons learned along the way.

My hobbies

Taekwon-Do ITF

Taekwon-Do ITF has been a big part of my life for years. It’s not just a sport — it’s a way of thinking, a discipline that shapes both the body and the mind. Through training, I’ve learned patience, focus, and respect — not only for others but also for myself. I’ve competed in numerous tournaments, constantly pushing my limits and learning from every fight. Each training session teaches me something new about perseverance and self-control. Taekwon-Do is more than breaking boards or winning medals. It’s about inner strength, confidence, and the drive to improve every single day. That same mindset helps me in coding, studying, and life — to stay sharp, calm, and always ready for the next challenge.

Reading books

For me, reading isn't just a pastime; it's a way to understand the systems that shape our world. I'm drawn to non-fiction, especially books on strategy, psychology, and financial literacy. Authors like Robert Kiyosaki ("Rich Dad Poor Dad") and Robert Greene ("The 48 Laws of Power") have trained me to look at challenges from a different, more strategic angle. I believe the principles in these books are universal. Understanding human motivation and long-term planning is just as critical in software development as it is in life. Building a great IT product requires thinking several steps ahead and truly understanding the end-user—skills I sharpen with every book I read. I see it as mental training that complements my physical discipline, helping me achieve my goals both in code and beyond.
